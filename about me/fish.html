IM FISH

Fish are aquatic, cold-blooded vertebrates that live in water and breathe through gills. They are one of the most diverse groups of animals, with over 30,000 known species found in oceans, rivers, lakes, and streams worldwide. Fish vary greatly in size, color, and shape, from tiny guppies to massive whales like the whale shark.

Fish are adapted to their watery environment with streamlined bodies, fins for swimming, and gills for extracting oxygen from water. They are an essential part of the aquatic ecosystem, playing a role as predators and prey in the food chain. Their diets range from plankton and algae to smaller fish, depending on the species.

Many fish, such as salmon and tuna, are also a vital source of food for humans. They are rich in nutrients like omega-3 fatty acids, making them an important part of a healthy diet.

Fish are also kept as pets, with species like goldfish and bettas being popular in home aquariums. Whether admired for their beauty or their ecological importance, fish are fascinating creatures that thrive in the underwater world.
